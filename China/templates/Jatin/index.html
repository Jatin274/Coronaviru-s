Knowledge about COVID-19

Corona Virus

In 2019, the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC) started monitoring the outbreak of a new coronavirus, SARS-CoV-2, which causes the respiratory illness now known as COVID-19. Authorities first identified the virus in Wuhan, China. More than 78,191 people have contracted the virus in China. Health authorities have identified many other people with COVID-19 around the world, including in the United States. On January 31, 2020, the virus passed from one person to another in the U.S.

The World Health Organization (WHO) have declared a public health emergency relating to COVID-19.

Since then, this strain has been diagnosed in several U.S. residents. The CDC have advised that it is likely to spread to more people. COVID-19 has started causing disruption in at least 25 other countries. read more in WHO report Of India-...

how does corona spreads

infected person cough

When an infected person coughs or sneezes he/she transmits the droplets into the atmosphere. From where these droplets come in contact with other people and infects them too. It’s better to use a cloth or handkerchief while sneezing this avoids containment of infection droplets.

person to person

The virus most often spreads through people who have symptoms. But it may be possible to pass it on without showing any signs. Some people who don't know they've been infected can give it to others. This is called asympto -matic spread. You can also pass it on before you notice any signs of infection, called presymptomatic spread.

close contact

This can happen in two ways: Direct close contact: one can get the infection by being in close contact with COVID-19 especially if they do not cover their face when coughing or sneezing. Indirect close contact: the droplets survive on surfaces and clothes for many days.

symptoms of corona

People with COVID-19 generally develop signs and symptoms, including mild respiratory symptoms and fever, on an average of 5-6 days after infection (mean incubation period 5-6 days, range 1-14 days). Most people infected with COVID-19 virus have mild disease and recover.

  • Cough
  • Fever
  • Breath shortness
  • Tiredness
  • Headache
  • Muscle pain

Prevention from corona

To prevent the spread of COVID-19: Clean your hands often. Use soap and water, or an alcohol-based hand rub. Maintain a safe distance from anyone who is coughing or sneezing. Don’t touch your eyes, nose or mouth. Stay home if you feel unwell. If you have a fever, cough and difficulty breathing, seek medical attention. Follow the directions of your local health authority.
